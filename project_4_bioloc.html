Mobile Robot Localization via Brain-Inspired Approach

This project was done as my final year project of bachelor.

Credit: Jiale Fan

Abstract

Brain-inspired localization algorithms have many advantages, such as robustness to dynamic environments, the ability to correct accumulated errors that may lead to localization failure, and the suitability to localize using low-resolution sensors. However, the existing algorithms are vulnerable in the presence of similar appearances, which may result in false re-localization, thus limiting their applicable scenarios. Based on the study of existing brain-inspired localization algorithms, this paper proposes a method of adaptive feature extraction using the neural network structure named variational autoencoder, which notably reduces the size of templates and enhances its adaptability to different environments. Further, a new algorithm flow for path integration and the selection of energy injection points of continuous attractor network based on odometric information, is also proposed. It effectively tackles the problem that it’s difficult for brain-inspired SLAM algorithm to distinguish indoor places with similar appearance and improves the metric accuracy of experience map. By comparing the mapping results of proposed algorithm and RatSLAM used as baseline, it is demonstrated that proposed algorithm can localize more robustly in environments with ambiguous visual scenes while using less space for template storage. The topological relationship of experience map constructed by proposed algorithm is highly consistent with that in the real world, and has metric accuracy of human-understandable level. It’s also confirmed that the baseline RatSLAM can not achieve the effect of proposed algorithm by tuning parameters, which shows the necessity and effectiveness of our modifications.
